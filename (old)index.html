StudentSpot

Thinking about classes? Need help with planning? Welcome to StudentSpot.

StudentSpot was created to give students help with planning, scheduling, etc. Only other students can really tell you what different classes are like.

After realizing this, we recruited a team of other students to create a place that student could learn from their peers about classes. After months of progress, the finished product was then named StudentSpot.

Find out what classes are like before you take them.

Subject: Math/IT

Advanced Computer Mathematics has a focus to provide the student with a conceptual background in computer science. Topics include computer architecture, data representation, operating systems, computing systems in society, and software development. Students will implement the major stages of software development using a high level language. Topics will include loops, selections, and arrays. This advanced course covers all topics in the regular Computer Mathematics class as well as others. In some schools this course is the first year of a three-year curriculum in Computer Science.

Teacher Feedback:

Work Time:

Students should expect to spend 30 minutes each week for this class.

Prerequisites:

Required: Algebra 1; Currently enrolled in Geometry or higher
Recommended:None

Useful Skills and Advice

As an advanced course a student should never fall behind. Every topic for the entire year is used for the rest of the year - getting behind in any topic means the student cannot succeed for the rest of the year. Quizzes make up the bulk of the grade, however the majority of time in class will be based on smaller tasks or projects, all which are used to have students repeatedly make mistakes and learn until things make sense. If a student decides to rely too heavily on other individuals to get through the smaller tasks or projects, they will not be able to succeed on the quizzes. This is one of the most entertaining courses that exists, but you cannot fall into the trap that just because it is entertaining that it is easy.

What will I Learn?

This is an introductory course on how to write code to program software. We work in Java as the primary language to learn most of the basics of how programs are written. The course should be called PreAP Computer Science, as it leads directly into the AP year, covering approximately 75% of the material on the AP exam, but not quite at the depth of the AP level. Students will learn how to: work with graphics, make objects move around, be controlled by keyboard and mouse, interact with other objects, build subroutines, control when and where code gets triggered, learn the basics of looping, nested looping, arrays, strings, number systems, how colors work in software, and various other topics.

How will this course benefit me?

Aside from becoming a software programmer as a potential career path, even if a student decides to not go further into computer science they will have gained vital knowledge of how to better use a computer and problem solving skills that can be applied to any field.

Subject: Science

Pre-AP Chemistry provides the highly motivated, college-bound student with a rigorous first-year chemistry course. Students who elect to take this course must have a true desire to take AP Chemistry as a second-year course. The content of this course includes the following: matter and energy, atomic structure, bonding, periodic table, mathematics of chemistry, kinetics and equilibrium, acids and bases, redox and electrochemistry, organic chemistry, applications of chemical properties, nuclear chemistry, and laboratory activities. All content objectives are extended and enriched. Students will complete laboratory activities that emphasize the quantitative applications learned in class. Students are required to take the Chemistry Standards of Learning assessment at the end of this course.

Teacher Feedback:

Work Time:

Students should expect to spend 2-3 hours each week for this class.

Prerequisites:

Required: Algebra 1 (with at least B or B+)
Recommended Corequisite: at least Algebra 2 (you WILL struggle if you are only in geometry at the same time as Pre-AP Chemistry)

Useful Skills and Advice

Pre-AP Chemistry requires a much stronger math foundation than general Chemistry 1. PAP students also need strong logic/critical thinking skills to enable them to think on their own and understand higher level concepts. Pre-AP Chemistry is an extremely difficult course. For many students, it will be one of the most challenging classes they take in high school. We apply math in deeper ways than you've ever been required to do in other classes, while also learning higher-level science concepts. It is not abnormal to struggle in this class, even if you're never struggled in other classes before. As long as you are willing to work hard, ask questions when you're confused, and stay after for extra help when you need it, you should be fine. (And remember, it's okay to get less than an A in this class. Colleges recognize the difficulty level of Pre-AP Chemistry and think highly of students for persevering thought it.) Don't be scared of taking this class, but also don't go into it blind to the effort that you will need to put in, even if school has traditionally been easy for you up to this point.

What will I Learn?

Scientific measurement, Thermochemistry, Atomic structure, The periodic table, Bonding, Reactions, Stoichiometry, Solutions, Gases, Nuclear and Organic chemistry

How will this course benefit me?

Chemistry teaches problem solving and critical thinking skills, which are important in every career. Specific career paths opened by chemistry are medicine, pharmacology, research, engineering, forensic science, food sciences (yes, cooking is chemistry!), environmental science, patent law, agriculture, nanotechnology, the development of plastics, electronics, beauty products, and more. It also helps students develop scientific literacy, so they are better able to identify science myths perpetuated by social media. Pre-AP Chemistry also leads very well into taking AP Chemistry the subsequent year.

Subject: Science

Advanced Placement Biology is designed to be the equivalent of a first year introduction college biology course. AP Biology is designed for students who have successfully completed foundation courses in biology and chemistry. This course aims to provide students with the conceptual framework, factual knowledge, and analytical skills necessary to deal critically with the rapidly changing science of biology. Primary emphasis of the course is on developing an understanding of concepts rather than on memorizing terms and technical details. At least 25% of instructional time is dedicated to hands-on laboratory investigations. Students are expected to take the Advanced Placement Biology examination in May. With a satisfactory score, students may be eligible to receive some credit for college-level biology.

Teacher Feedback:

Work Time:

Students should expect to spend 3-4 hours each week for this class.

Prerequisites:

Required: 9th grade Biology with a B or better Algebra 1 with a B or better Chemistry with a B or better
Recommended: N/A

Useful Skills and Advice

Students must be self-disciplined in order to be successful in AP Biology and also must have a strong interest in science. The course requires students to complete readings, practice activities, watch video lectures, etc. that are not necessarily graded for class credit, but are required as exposure and extra practice to gain mastery of content. Students should also possess time management skills and be aware of the balance necessary be successful in AP courses in conjunction with other courses and activities.

What will I Learn?

Unit 1: Science Process Skills (review of basic science concept skills, graphing, basic statistics/math, using common scientific equipment, making measurements, etc.) Unit 2: Evolution (mechanisms and types of evolution, evidence of evolution, analysis of evolutionary relationships) Unit 3: Biochemistry (evaluation of the chemical properties, structure, and resulting function of biological molecules, emphasis on proteins and enzymes) Unit 4: Cellular Structure and Function (review and expansion on the components of the eukaryotic cell, in depth exploration of cellular membrane components and chemical structure as well as transport of materials across the membrane) Unit 5: Cellular Energetics (chemical and physical processes related to photosynthesis and cellular respiration) Unit 6: Cell Cycle and Regulation (review and expansion on chemical and physical processes related to mitosis and meiosis) Unit 7: Mendelian Genetics (review of traditional Mendelian genetics, advanced probability and statistics, multi-gene crosses) Unit 8: Molecular Genetics and Biotechnology (DNA replication/transcription/translation, restriction enzymes, bioengineering, plasmids) Unit 9: Kingdoms of Life (in depth study of bacteria and bacterial components, overview in comparing/contrasting the kingdoms of life) Unit 10: Human Systems (small and large-scale mechanisms, signaling, etc. of the following body systems: cardiovascular systems, immune system, nervous system, endocrine system, and developmental processes) Unit 11: Ecology (review of ecology basics, expansion into statistical analysis in population ecology, ecological dynamics) Unit 12: AP Test Review

How will this course benefit me?

AP Biology affords students the opportunity to evaluate the preparedness of their science skills for college-level courses. The content of AP Biology is equivalent to that of a 100-level university course and students who are successful in the AP Biology gain both the equivalent content knowledge but also have the opportunity earn the equivalency college credit via the AP Test for the course. This course will provide students insight into their aptitude for science-based majors and careers, particularly those who are interested in pre-medical/dental/pharm/veterinary. Skills gained in this course include: -Basic content knowledge equivalent to successful completion of a 100-level university course in introductory biology -Development of advanced laboratory and scientific inquiry skills -Ability to process data via basic statistical analysis to determine validity and significance -Increased reading comprehension of technical documents, such as scientific literature

Pre-AP Biology is a lab-based course designed to give students a foundation in biological concepts as well as the opportunity to apply principles of experimental design in laboratory settings and on a required student project. Pre-AP Biology includes the same major areas of study as Biology I but with extensions to each curriculum objective and associated specialty program. It is useful for students to have the following skills prior to entering the class: -Basic math skills including multiplication, division, fractions, calculation of percentages, etc. -Reading comprehension in which a student is able to read a passage and extract the essential information -Basic graphing skills such as appropriately determining when data should be displayed in a bar graph v a line graph, labeling of axises. Pre-AP Biology requires the following beyond that of regular Biology 1: additional reading/writing and math application throughout the school year, more in-depth hands-on activities and labs with extended analysis/post-labs, additional out of class assignments such as reading or projects, as well as students will receive extended content instruction for each unit that goes beyond SOL standards and assessments will require more higher order thinking to this affect.

What will I Learn?

Unit 1: Science Process Skills (graphing, basic statistics/math, using common scientific equipment, making measurements, etc.) Unit 2: Ecology (ecological organization, interactions between organisms, population ecology, components of ecosystems, food chains/webs) Unit 3: Biochemistry (basic chemistry including bonding and chemical reactions, biological molecules, enzymes) Unit 4: Cellular Structure and Function (comparison of types of cells, exploration of eukaryotic cell components and functionality, components of the cell membrane and cellular transport) Unit 5: Cellular Processes (photosynthesis, cellular respiration, cell cycle, cellular reproduction: mitosis and meiosis) Unit 6: Protein Synthesis (history and structure of DNA and RNA, transcription/translation, universal genetic code) Unit 7: Genetics (modes of inheritance, Mendelian genetics and Punnett squares, non-Mendelian genetics and Punnett squares, probability) Unit 8: Evolution (natural selection, evidence for evolution) Unit 9: Taxonomy (kingdoms of life, comparison of eukaryotic organisms) Unit 10: SOL Review Unit 11: Post-SOL Materials (content that appears in standards but is not tested on by SOL test: survey of human body systems and processes)

How will this course benefit me?

Pre-AP Biology is students' first opportunity to gain exposure to advanced science curriculum at the high school level. Students who are successful in Pre-AP biology will likely be better prepared for other Pre-AP and AP sciences. The content of the course is a rudimentary level survey of the type of material that a pre-medical/dental/pharm/veterinary student would take across their major courses for an undergraduate degree, which may help students develop interests in these fields. Some skills gained in this course are: -Science process skills that build upon previous learning including statistical analysis, creating and reading graphs, and collecting accurate measurements -Basic understanding of components of life, types of living things, and their interactions including disease processes -Experience conducting hands on laboratory exercises that will provide foundational skills for future science classes in high school and beyond
